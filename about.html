I work on Linux-based embedded systems with a focus on camera pipelines, video streaming, and system-level integration. Over time, my work has evolved from building features to designing complete data paths — from sensor capture to reliable delivery across applications and cloud systems.

My focus is on how data flows through systems — across kernel, user space, and network layers — and how each stage can be optimised for latency, throughput, and stability.

I approach kernel development hands-on: the Driver Lab documents seven progressively complex drivers I built to understand the primitives that underpin real camera pipeline implementations — ring buffers, wait queues, spinlocks, softirq context, workqueues, and real hardware GPIO on BeagleBone Black.
